What is the Travel Document?

Official document · MPPRE · Venezuela Foreign Ministry

Travel Document / Safe-Conduct Pass

The Travel Document (also known as a Safe-Conduct Pass) is issued by Venezuela's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MPPRE) through its official portal, and authorizes a Venezuelan citizen to enter the country when they do not have a valid passport.

The process is done online directly through the Venezuelan Foreign Ministry's portal. XploraTrip guides you through every step so your application goes through on the first try.

🌐 Official portal · documentodeviaje.mppre.gob.ve

Need help with the process? Message us and we will do it together →

General requirements (Safe-Conduct Pass)

  • ✓Venezuelan ID card (can be expired)
  • ✓Venezuelan passport (even if expired, it works for identification)
  • ✓Justification for the reason you are returning
  • ✓Contact information in Venezuela
  • ✓Current address in the USA
Requirements vary based on your situation. We advise you on your specific case — there is no one-size-fits-all process.

The DEV (Travel Document) is issued by the MPPRE (Venezuela's Ministry of Foreign Affairs) for Venezuelans who want to return without a valid passport. You need it if your passport has been expired for a long time, you lost it, or you only have your ID card. We guide you through the entire process.
Under normal conditions, 2 to 6 weeks from when the application is submitted on the official portal documentodeviaje.mppre.gob.ve. The time depends on the MPPRE's application volume. Urgent humanitarian cases can be processed faster — we help make sure your application is complete on the first try.
The DEV is designed to authorize your entry into Venezuela when you don't have a valid passport — it doesn't replace a passport for leaving the country again. If you plan to return to the USA afterward, we'll explain what you need for that leg based on your immigration status.
In general, a child born in the USA can travel to Venezuela with a valid U.S. passport, just like any U.S. citizen. Venezuelan documents aren't required for the trip. If you also want the Ministry to recognize them as Venezuelan since they're a child of Venezuelan parents, that's a separate, voluntary process — we'll explain both paths during your consultation.
Yes. Venezuela recognizes children of a Venezuelan mother or father born abroad as Venezuelan citizens. Registration is done at a Venezuelan consulate and is not a requirement for travel — it's a family decision about the child's future nationality. We can guide you through the process if you're interested.
U.S. citizens, including minors, generally don't need a visa for short tourist stays in Venezuela. We recommend confirming current requirements before your trip, since entry policies can change — we'll review this with you during your consultation.
Yes, direct routes exist from cities like Miami to Caracas and other destinations, though availability changes by season and airline. If there's no direct flight from your city, we find the best connection — usually via Bogotá, Panama, or Miami.
A direct flight from Miami to Caracas takes about 3.5 to 4 hours. From other U.S. cities, total time depends on whether there's a layover. We'll give you the exact itinerary when we quote your route.
