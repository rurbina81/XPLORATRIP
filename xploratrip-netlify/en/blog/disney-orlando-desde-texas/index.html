Disney World from Texas 2026: Packages, Prices, and Tips for Families

Romer Urbina · XploraTrip July 2026 9 min read Plano, Texas

Disney World is the dream trip for thousands of Texas families — and for good reason. Four theme parks, dream hotels, and magic around every corner. From Plano, Dallas, or Houston you're less than 3 hours by air from Orlando. In this guide you'll find everything you need to plan your trip to Disney World in 2026: flights, tickets, hotels, a real budget, and the tips that make the difference between an exhausting visit and an experience your family will remember for a lifetime.

✨ Advantage from Texas: Orlando is closer to Dallas than many Caribbean destinations. Direct flights in 2h 40min from DFW. You don't need a visa or any additional paperwork if your immigration status in the U.S. is already in order.

Why is Disney World the ultimate family trip?

Disney World isn't just a theme park — it's the largest entertainment complex in the world. Spanning more than 40 square miles in Orlando, it includes 4 main theme parks, 2 water parks, more than 30 official hotels, and Disney Springs, a shopping and dining area with no entry fee. The magic is in the details: characters who greet you by name, shows that stop the whole park in its tracks, and attractions designed for every age.

  • For young kids (2-7 years): Magic Kingdom is paradise. Fantasyland, Cinderella Castle, the parades. A transformative experience.
  • For teens: Hollywood Studios with Galaxy's Edge (Star Wars) and EPCOT with attractions for adults.
  • For adults: EPCOT World Showcase, signature restaurant dinners, spas, golf, and nightly entertainment.

The 4 Disney World parks

Each park has its own identity and requires at least a full day to see it properly:

Magic Kingdom — The heart of Disney

The most-visited park in the world and the most iconic. Cinderella Castle at the end of Main Street U.S.A. is the image everyone recognizes. The areas include Fantasyland (carousel, It's a Small World, under the sea with Ariel), Adventureland (Pirates of the Caribbean), Frontierland, and Tomorrowland. The 3pm parade and the “Disney Enchantment” fireworks at 9pm are must-sees. Best day for families with young kids.

EPCOT — Culture, science, and food

The most underrated park and adults’ favorite. World Showcase has 11 “countries” with authentic food, drink, and culture from Canada, the UK, France, Japan, Mexico, and more. World Discovery and World Nature feature technology and nature attractions. Guardians of the Galaxy: Cosmic Rewind is the park’s newest and most spectacular roller coaster.

Hollywood Studios — Action and adventure

For fans of Star Wars, Indiana Jones, Toy Story, and the Avengers. Star Wars: Galaxy's Edge (with Millennium Falcon and Rise of the Resistance) is the most immersive attraction in the Disney world. Slinky Dog Dash for the little ones. The Indiana Jones show and Tower of Terror for the bravest.

Animal Kingdom — Nature and adventure

More than a zoo — a safari park with Pandora (Avatar) as its crown jewel. Flight of Passage (the 3D/4D banshee flight over Pandora) consistently ranks among the best attractions in the world. Kilimanjaro Safari puts you face to face with real giraffes, elephants, and rhinos. Ideal as a second or third day.

Lightning Lane: For families with kids, the Lightning Lane Multi Pass (formerly FastPass) is almost mandatory at Magic Kingdom and Hollywood Studios. It lets you skip the longest lines with reservations from the app. The extra spend can save you 2-3 hours of waiting in a day.

Hotels: inside or outside the park?

Official Disney hotels

Disney has more than 30 hotels of its own, grouped into Value, Moderate, Deluxe, and Deluxe Villa categories. Exclusive perks include Early Theme Park Entry (30 minutes before the general public), free transportation between parks via Magical Express, and priority access to Lightning Lane reservations. The most popular for families on a moderate budget is Disney's Pop Century Resort (from ~$180/night).

Hotels outside the park (off-site)

International Drive and Lake Buena Vista have hundreds of hotels 10-20 minutes from the park, with rates from $90/night. Marriott, Hilton, and Hyatt have excellent properties at half the price. The downside: no Disney perks, and you need your own transportation or Uber. For families with older kids who don't need the extra magic, it's the smarter choice.

When to go to Disney World from Texas?

  • January–February (except MLK and Presidents' Day): The least crowded days of the year. Shorter lines, pleasant weather (64-77°F). The best time to go if you can pull the kids out of school.
  • Easter week (March-April): Very high demand. High prices and packed parks. Only if you have no other option.
  • May–June (before June 15): Good balance. Warm weather, fewer crowds than summer. Moderate prices.
  • July–August: Texas summer high season. Extreme heat (95°F+), high humidity, maximum crowds. If you go, arrive at park opening and rest at the hotel from 1-4pm.
  • September–October (except Halloween): Low season, excellent prices. The After Hours Halloween Party is a very popular paid special event.
  • Christmas: The most magical time but also the most expensive and crowded. Decorations, special shows, and the Castle lit up in a special way.

Essential tips for Disney from Texas

  • Download the My Disney Experience app: Book Lightning Lane, see real-time wait times, locate characters, and more. It's essential.
  • Pack clothing and sunscreen: Florida's summer heat is different from Texas's. Lightweight clothing, comfortable shoes, and high-SPF sunscreen are a must.
  • Eat breakfast before you go in: Eating outside the park in the morning and inside at midday saves you $20-30 per person.
  • Mondays and Tuesdays are the least crowded days: Especially at the less popular parks. Avoid Saturdays and Sundays.
  • Book dinner reservations ahead: The best restaurants like Be Our Guest (Beauty and the Beast) or Cinderella's Royal Table book up 60 days in advance. Log into the system at 6am ET for the best time slots.
  • Bring Mickey ears: Disney culture — everyone wears their ears. Buying them on Amazon before the trip is 3 times cheaper than in the park.

Combine Disney with Miami: the complete Florida trip

If you have 7 or more days available, you can combine Disney World with Miami in the same trip and see two of Florida's best cities with no extra layover from Texas. The distance between Orlando and Miami is about 3.5 hours via the I-4 / Florida's Turnpike, which makes the drive perfectly manageable in a rental car.

The most popular route: fly to Orlando → 4 days at Disney World → rent a car → drive to Miami → 2-3 days exploring the city → fly back to Texas from Miami (MIA). The one-way car rental between Orlando and Miami costs $30-60 extra over a standard rental. This combination lets you enjoy Disney magic with the kids and end the trip with beach, food, and nightlife in Miami for the adults. See the complete Miami from Texas guide →

Reverse alternative: Start with 2-3 days in Miami (beach + Wynwood + Little Havana), then drive to Orlando for the parks. That way the trip ends with the excitement of Disney — perfect if you're traveling with kids who'll be counting down the days from the moment you land.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a Disney World trip for a family of 4 from Texas cost?

A 4-day trip (flight + hotel + tickets) for 2 adults and 2 kids from Dallas or Houston costs between $3,200 and $7,000 depending on the hotel and season. The most affordable option is flying Spirit/Frontier, staying off-site, and buying 4-day tickets in low season.

How many days do I need to see Disney World?

To see the 4 main parks with enough time you need at least 4 days (one park per day). If you want to revisit Magic Kingdom or add a water park, plan for 5-6 days. With 2-3 days you could rush through 2-3 parks.

Do I need a visa to go to Disney World from Texas?

No. Disney World is in Orlando, Florida — within the United States. If you're already in the U.S. with a valid visa or residency, you don't need any additional paperwork. You just need valid ID for the airport.

How long is the flight from Texas to Orlando?

Direct flights from Dallas/Fort Worth take about 2 hours 40 minutes. From Houston it's about 2 hours 30 minutes. There are multiple daily direct flights with American, Southwest, Spirit, and Frontier.

Is it better to buy Disney tickets online or at the entrance?

Always buy online and in advance. At the gate they're more expensive, and tickets can sell out during high season. Buy at disneyworld.com and add the Lightning Lane Multi Pass from the app on the day of your visit.
