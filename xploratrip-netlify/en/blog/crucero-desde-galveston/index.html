MSC Seascape Cruise from Galveston: 7 Nights to the Western Caribbean

Romer Urbina · XploraTrip Jul–Oct 2026 Updated Jul 6, 2026 8 min read Departs from Galveston, TX

You can board the MSC Seascape directly in Galveston, with no flight required — one of the most impressive cruise ships sailing out of Texas. Over 7 nights, this cruise takes you to three Western Caribbean destinations: Costa Maya in Mexico, the incredible Roatán Island in Honduras, and iconic Cozumel. A trip that combines sea days, white-sand beaches, coral reefs, and all the luxury experience MSC has to offer.

Did you know? Galveston is the 4th-largest cruise port in the U.S. and has excellent connectivity from all over Texas — just 4 hours from Dallas, 50 minutes from Houston. Perfectly manageable by car or bus.

🇻🇪 For the Venezuelan and Latino community in Texas: MSC Cruises is a European-origin cruise line with a strong presence in Latin America — it's probably the cruise line where you'll most easily find Spanish-speaking staff on board, familiar menus, and an atmosphere that feels comfortable from day one. The itinerary through Costa Maya, Roatán, and Cozumel also includes stops where Spanish is the language of the day, prices on land are accessible, and the local food is spectacular.

The MSC Seascape: the ship

The MSC Seascape is a World Class-class ship from MSC Cruises, with capacity for more than 5,800 passengers. It launched in 2022 and is one of the most modern ships in the MSC fleet. Highlights on board:

  • 21 decks with multiple pools, tracks, and entertainment areas
  • 14 restaurants (several specialty ones at extra cost, the main one included)
  • 19 bars and lounges including the famous Cocktail Bar with ocean views
  • Sky trail track suspended over the ocean, outdoor zipline, and water slides
  • MSC Aurea Spa — spa and wellness area with ocean views
  • MSC Casino on board, main theater with nightly shows and live entertainment
  • Family zone with an exclusive kids' area and Youth Center programs
  • Butler service in MSC Yacht Club cabins (top class)

Costa Maya, Mexico

Costa Maya is the least-known destination on the itinerary — and that's precisely what makes it special. Unlike Cozumel, it isn't packed with cruise passengers. The port itself has a tourist zone with restaurants, shops, and activities, but the real gem is the nearly pristine coral reef just offshore and the fishing village of Mahahual. If you're interested, Bacalar Lagoon — known as the "Lake of Seven Colors" — is 2 hours away by taxi and is one of the most spectacular places in Mexico.

Roatán Island, Honduras

Roatán is the jewel of the Honduran Caribbean and one of the best diving destinations in the world. The island is part of the Mesoamerican Reef System, the second-longest coral barrier on the planet. The ship docks at Mahogany Bay, a private pier with its own beach. West Bay Beach, a 15-minute taxi ride away, has crystal-clear turquoise water and fine white sand. The snorkeling here is literally breathtaking — the corals and fish are just steps from the shore. For those seeking adrenaline, there's zip-lining over the sea.

Cozumel, Mexico

Cozumel is the most popular port in the Mexican Caribbean, and there's a simple reason: it has everything. For sea lovers, Palancar Reef is considered one of the best diving sites in the world and sits right off the island. Chankanaab National Park offers snorkeling, dolphin encounters, and a beach area. For shoppers, downtown San Miguel has hundreds of shops with jewelry, tequila, crafts, and souvenirs. And the margaritas here are legendary.

How to get from Dallas to Galveston

Galveston is 4 hours from Dallas via I-45 South. These are the most popular options:

  • Driving your own car: The most convenient option. There are parking lots near the port in Galveston (Galveston Cruise Terminal) from $10-15/day. Book ahead during high season.
  • Bus + ferry: Companies like Greyhound or FlixBus have Dallas–Houston routes, and from Houston there are taxis or Uber to Galveston (approx. 1 hour, $40-60).
  • Fly to Houston + transfer: If you're coming from a distant city, fly to Houston IAH or HOU and take a taxi/Uber or shuttle to Galveston. There are direct shuttle services from the Houston airports.
  • The night before: Many passengers prefer to arrive the day before and stay in Galveston or near the port to avoid stress on boarding day. Galveston has good oceanfront hotels.

Logistics tip: On boarding day, lines to board start at 10am. If you arrive before noon you can get on the ship, eat at the restaurants, and start exploring while the rest of the passengers arrive. The cabin is generally ready by 2pm.

What's included in the cruise price?

  • Cabin accommodations (7 nights)
  • Breakfast, lunch, and dinner at the ship's main restaurant
  • 24-hour buffet (snacks and alternative breakfast)
  • Onboard entertainment (shows, theater, activities)
  • Access to pools, hot tubs, and deck areas
  • Access to table tennis courts, mini-golf, and sports activities
  • Port fees (included in the price we quote)

Not included: drinks (there are unlimited drink packages at a fixed price), shore excursions, specialty onboard restaurants, spa, cruise photos, gratuities (MSC automatically charges a gratuity of ~$14/day/person).

Tips for your cruise from Galveston

  • Drink package: If you drink more than 3-4 drinks a day, the unlimited drink package is worth it. Ask us for the current price when you get a quote.
  • Port excursions: You can book directly with MSC (more expensive) or with local operators at each port (cheaper and similar quality). We can guide you.
  • Onboard Wi-Fi: It's expensive but packages are available. Many passengers choose to unplug completely — it's part of the experience!
  • Formal night: MSC has at least one formal gala night. Bring something elegant — the experience is worth it and the photos are spectacular.
  • Gratuities: Automatically charged to your onboard account (~$14/person/night). They're not optional in most cases with MSC.
  • Cash at the ports: Bring small-denomination dollar bills for tips and local purchases at the ports. Credit cards are accepted at most shops.

Is this your first cruise? What no one tells you

If you've never been on a cruise, there are things people take for granted that are worth knowing before you board:

  • The ship card is everything: When you board you're given a card (or wristband) that works as your cabin key, onboard payment method, and ID at every port. Always carry it with you.
  • The ship won't wait for you: If you miss the return time at any port, the ship sails without you. MSC clearly states return times; always respect them and build in a 30-45 minute buffer.
  • Ship account ≠ cash: On board, almost everything is paid with the ship card (charged to your account). On land, cash (dollars) and credit cards are preferable. Notify your bank before the trip that you'll be leaving the country.
  • Seasickness isn't inevitable: The MSC Seascape is a large ship — the motion in calm seas is minimal. If you're prone to seasickness, bring dimenhydrinate tablets (Dramamine) as a precaution. Sea days in the Caribbean tend to be very calm.
  • Book specialty restaurants when you board: Specialty restaurants (extra cost) fill up in the first days. If you want to dine at the sushi restaurant or the premium grill, book as soon as you get on the ship.
  • Your suitcase arrives later: When you board, your checked luggage is delivered to the cabin hours later (up to 7pm). Pack a change of clothes, documents, and medications in your carry-on.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a passport for the cruise from Galveston?

Yes, it's highly recommended. Although technically U.S. citizens can use a birth certificate + ID on roundtrip cruises from the U.S., MSC and practically all cruise lines strongly recommend bringing a valid passport. If you're Latin American (Venezuelan, Colombian, etc.), a valid passport is mandatory.

How much does the MSC Seascape cruise from Galveston cost?

Prices for August–October 2026 departures start from $409 per person in an interior cabin (double occupancy). Prices vary by date and availability — October tends to be the most affordable season. Get a quote with us for the exact price on your preferred date.

Can I book just the cruise without a flight?

Yes. If you already have a way to get to Galveston, we can quote you just the cruise. If you want a package that includes a flight to Houston + transfer to Galveston + cruise, we can organize that too.

How early before departure should I arrive at the port?

MSC recommends arriving at least 3 hours before departure (which is usually at 5pm, so arrive at the port before 3:30pm). Boarding starts at 10am. The earlier you arrive, the more time you have to explore the ship before it starts filling up.
