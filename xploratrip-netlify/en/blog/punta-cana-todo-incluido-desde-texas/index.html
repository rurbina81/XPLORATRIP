Punta Cana All-Inclusive from Texas 2026: Complete Guide and Real Prices

Romer Urbina · XploraTrip July 2026 8 min read Plano, Texas

Punta Cana is the most-visited beach destination in the Caribbean — for a very simple reason: movie-perfect white-sand beaches, flawless turquoise water, and all-inclusive resorts that mean you don't have to think about a thing. From Texas it's one of the most accessible trips in both time and price, with direct flights from Dallas and Houston. In this guide we give you everything you need to plan your trip to Punta Cana in 2026.

Quick fact: From Dallas you can reach Punta Cana in about 4 hours on a direct flight. All-inclusive packages start from $750 per person (flight + hotel, 5 nights, low season). Ideal for couples, families, and honeymoons.

Why choose Punta Cana?

Punta Cana has something few Caribbean destinations can match: an enormous concentration of high-quality all-inclusive resorts in a relatively compact area. That means you can compare options across every price range and find exactly what you're looking for, from budget-friendly family resorts to ultra-luxury properties.

  • World-class beaches: Bávaro and Playa Blanca are consistently ranked among the best beaches in the world. Fine white sand, warm water without strong surf.
  • No visa for U.S. residents: If you hold a valid U.S. visa or Green Card, you enter the Dominican Republic with no additional paperwork.
  • Direct flights from Texas: American Airlines, JetBlue, and Spirit fly direct from Dallas and Houston.
  • Competitive prices: The concentration of resorts creates real competition, with better prices than destinations like Jamaica or Aruba.
  • 24/7 entertainment: Nightly shows, pool activities, water sports, spa — all included on the wristband.

The best areas of Punta Cana

Punta Cana isn't just one place — it's a region with several areas, each with its own personality:

Bávaro — The most popular

This is the heart of touristic Punta Cana. Miles of beach lined with dozens of all-inclusive resorts side by side. Ideal for families and anyone who wants constant activity. This is where you'll find Iberostar, Royalton, Hard Rock, and Majestic. The best value in the whole region.

Cap Cana — The most exclusive

A gated luxury community south of Bávaro. Less bustling, more private beaches, and upscale resorts (Juanillo, Hyatt Ziva). If your budget allows and you want tranquility with luxury, this is your area.

Macao — The most authentic

An undeveloped beach with no massive resorts. Perfect for anyone who wants to see Punta Cana beyond the all-inclusive bubble. We use it as a day trip, not as a home base.

Requirements to enter the Dominican Republic

This is the most frequent question from Venezuelan and Latin American travelers from Texas:

  • Valid passport: Required for everyone. It must be valid for the duration of your stay.
  • Do I need a visa if I have a U.S. visa? No. Holders of a valid U.S. visa or Green Card enter the Dominican Republic without an additional visa. This is one of the reasons Punta Cana is so popular among Venezuelans in the U.S.
  • Tourist card: It used to be paid separately; now it's included in most flights arriving at PUJ.
  • Customs declaration: Filled out on the in-flight form.

🇩🇴 Important for Venezuelans: You need a valid Venezuelan passport OR a passport from another country. If your Venezuelan passport is expired but you have a valid U.S. visa, the Dominican Republic will not allow you entry. Always check your passport's validity before buying your ticket.

Tips for your trip to Punta Cana from Texas

  • Best time to travel: December–April is high season (high prices, perfect weather). May–June and November have good rates with good weather. July–August is summer high season.
  • Bring cash in USD: The U.S. dollar is accepted at all resorts and most tourist businesses. Dominican pesos are useful for tips and local purchases.
  • Travel insurance: Always recommended. Many resorts require it or offer it as an add-on. Covers cancellations, medical emergencies, and lost luggage.
  • Book ahead: For Easter week, summer, and December, booking 3–4 months in advance can save you up to 25%.
  • Tipping: Not included in the all-inclusive rate. Carrying small $1 and $5 bills for hotel staff is standard practice.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a visa to go to Punta Cana if I have a U.S. visa?

No. Holders of a valid U.S. B1/B2 visa or Green Card (permanent residency) enter the Dominican Republic without an additional visa. You only need a passport that's valid for the duration of your stay.

How much does an all-inclusive package to Punta Cana from Texas cost?

A complete package (flight + transfers + all-inclusive hotel) for 7 nights costs between $1,140 and $2,850 per person depending on the resort and season. Direct flights from Dallas start from $220 round trip.

How long is the flight from Texas to Punta Cana?

The direct flight from Dallas/Fort Worth takes about 4 hours. From Houston it's about 3h 45min on a direct flight. Connections are available if the direct flight doesn't match your dates.

What's the difference between Punta Cana and Cancún?

Both are excellent destinations. Punta Cana has longer, quieter beaches, and resorts tend to offer better all-inclusive value. Cancún is livelier and has more cultural excursion options (Chichén Itzá, cenotes). From Texas, flights to Cancún are a bit cheaper and more frequent.
