How to Return to Venezuela from the USA in 2026: Complete Guide by Document Type

Romer Urbina · XploraTrip June 2026 Updated Jul 2, 2026 8 min read Plano, Texas

If you're a Venezuelan living in the United States and thinking about returning to Venezuela, one of the first questions that comes up is: can I travel with my current documents? The answer depends on your specific situation. In this article we explain the 4 most common situations and what to do in each case.

Important: This guide is informational, based on our experience handling real cases, and verified as of June 2026. Immigration policies can change. Always check your specific case with us before making decisions. The first consultation is completely free.

What is the MPPRE Travel Document?

The Travel Document is issued by Venezuela's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MPPRE) and allows Venezuelan citizens abroad to return to the country when they don't have a valid passport or can't easily renew it.

The process is handled through the official MPPRE portal: documentodeviaje.mppre.gob.ve

Processing time varies, but on average it takes between 2 and 6 weeks depending on the case and current demand.

Important: The MPPRE portal can be slow or show errors. If it's been more than 48 hours with no updates, message us on WhatsApp — we track your case and let you know instantly when there's movement.

The 4 situations: which one is yours?

🛂 Case 1: Valid Venezuelan passport Easiest

If your Venezuelan passport is valid (not expired), you have the most direct path. You can fly to Venezuela directly with that document.

What you need:

  • Venezuelan passport valid for at least 6 months (recommended)
  • One-way or round-trip plane ticket
  • In some cases, a background-check letter or additional documents depending on the airline

Our role: We book your flight at the best rates and advise you on each airline's specific requirements.

Case 2: Expired Venezuelan passport Manageable

An expired passport is one of the most common cases. There are routes that allow returning to Venezuela with an expired passport under certain conditions.

Options:

  • Passport renewal: If you can renew it at the nearest Venezuelan consulate, that's the ideal option. Wait times vary a lot by consulate.
  • MPPRE Travel Document: If you can't renew your passport, the Travel Document is the main alternative. It allows returning to Venezuela without a valid passport.
  • Some airlines accept expired passports: Depending on the route and airline, there may be options. We check case by case.

🪪 Case 3: Venezuelan ID only (no passport) Requires processing

This is the most common case among Venezuelans abroad. Many emigrated without renewing their passport or lost it. With only a Venezuelan ID card, the MPPRE Travel Document is the main path.

General steps:

  1. Register on the MPPRE portal
  2. Complete the Travel Document application form
  3. Submit the required documentation (ID card, photo, etc.)
  4. Wait for approval (2–6 weeks on average)
  5. Buy the ticket once the document is approved

We guide you through every step of the process and let you know when your document is ready so you can buy your flight.

🆘 Case 4: No Venezuelan documents Complex but possible

The most complex situation. Without a Venezuelan ID card or passport, the process requires identity verification with the MPPRE. It's not impossible, but it requires more time and effort.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a visa to enter Venezuela as a resident of the USA?

No. Venezuelan citizens do not need a visa to enter Venezuela. You only need a valid Venezuelan ID document (passport or MPPRE Travel Document).

Can I buy the ticket before getting the MPPRE Travel Document?

We don't recommend it. Airlines verify documentation at check-in and can deny boarding if you don't have the document. Wait until your document is approved before buying the ticket.

Which U.S. cities have flights to Venezuela?

Air connectivity to Venezuela is currently limited. The main departure points from the USA are Miami and New York. From Texas you'll generally need a connection. We check routes and prices based on your city.
