Miami from Texas 2026: Flights, What to Do, and Where to Stay

Get a Quote
Romer Urbina · XploraTrip July 2026 Updated Jul 6, 2026 8 min read Plano, Texas

You can get to Miami from Texas on a direct flight from Dallas or Houston in under 3 hours — one of the most vibrant cities in the United States, with white-sand beaches, world-class food, and a Latino community that makes it feel like home. In this guide you'll find everything you need to plan your trip to Miami in 2026: when to go, where to stay, what to do, how much it costs, and the tips tourists usually learn too late.

Miami is different from any other U.S. city: half Latin America, half cosmopolitan metropolis. If you're Venezuelan or Latin American in Texas, you're going to feel right at home — especially in Doral, the neighborhood with the largest concentration of Venezuelans outside Venezuela.

Why travel to Miami from Texas?

Miami combines beach, urban life, world-class shopping, food, and a strong Latino presence in a single destination. For travelers flying out of Dallas or Houston, it's one of the most accessible domestic trips in the country: a direct flight under 3 hours, dozens of daily departures, and prices that compete with any Caribbean destination.

But what makes Miami especially appealing for Venezuelans and Latin Americans in Texas is its atmosphere. In no other U.S. city will you find such cultural familiarity: restaurants with Spanish-language menus, neighborhoods where Spanish is the primary language, and a Latino community built over decades that welcomes you as one of its own.

It's also a city of very different experiences just a few miles apart: white-sand beach in the morning, contemporary art in the afternoon, one of the city's best restaurants at night, and snorkeling in the Keys or the Everglades the next day. Few cities in the U.S. pack so much variety into so little space.

The best Miami neighborhoods to visit

South Beach (SoBe)

Miami's tourist heart and the most famous in the world. Ocean Drive with its 1930s Art Deco buildings, iconic hotels, white-sand and turquoise beaches, and nightlife that kicks off after midnight. Ideal for those who want the full Miami experience: beach + party + fashion. It's the most expensive neighborhood to stay in, but the most convenient for tourists without a car.

Wynwood — Art and culture

The former industrial neighborhood turned into the epicenter of urban art worldwide. The Wynwood Walls murals are a must-see — artists from around the world repaint them every year. Dozens of galleries, fusion-cuisine restaurants, live-music bars, and emerging designer boutiques. The Saturday art market (Wynwood Market) is one of the best in the city. 15 minutes from South Beach by Uber.

Brickell — The Manhattan of the South

Miami's financial and business district, with the city's most modern skyscrapers. Perfect for those who prefer an executive hotel, sophisticated restaurants, and a more low-key nightlife. Brickell City Centre (luxury mall) and Mary Brickell Village have excellent dining options. The Metrorail connects Brickell directly to the airport.

Little Havana — Cuban and Latin American flavor

Calle Ocho is the main artery of this neighborhood with more than 60 years of Cuban history. Hand-rolled artisan cigars, dominoes in the parks, Cuban coffee at the walk-up windows, live music, and the best mojitos outside Havana. The Versailles Restaurant (open 24h) is an institution. Just 10 minutes from Brickell.

Doral — The Venezuelan city of the U.S.

If you're Venezuelan or Latin American, Doral is your neighborhood in Miami. With more than 50,000 Venezuelan residents, it's known as "Doralzuela" — you'll find arepas, cachapas, tequeños, Venezuelan brands, Venezuelan doctors, and even Venezuelan TV channels playing on restaurant screens. It's 20 minutes from MIA airport and 30 from South Beach.

Coconut Grove and Coral Gables

Miami's most elegant and quiet neighborhoods, perfect for families or those looking to escape the chaos of South Beach. Coconut Grove has a bohemian college-town vibe with open-air restaurants and boats in the bay. Coral Gables has the Venetian Pool (a public pool built in 1924 in a coral rock quarry) and the luxurious Biltmore Hotel.

What to do in Miami: the must-sees

  • South Beach: The most famous beach in the U.S. Lounge chair + umbrella cost $35-50/day. Streets 5th to 15th have the prettiest sand. Go Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday afternoons for fewer crowds.
  • Wynwood Walls: Free to visit (outdoor area). The indoor museum has an entry fee. Best on weekdays before 11am to avoid crowds and get good photos.
  • Biscayne National Park and the Keys: 45 min from Miami, coral reefs for snorkeling and diving. You can also day-trip to Key West (3h by car or Greyhound bus).
  • Cruise from PortMiami: The largest cruise port in the world departs from Biscayne Bay. Ideal for adding 3-5 Caribbean days to your Miami trip.
  • Design District: The luxury-brand neighborhood (Louis Vuitton, Hermès, Chanel) with public art installations. Even if you don't buy anything, it feels like an open-air gallery.
  • Little Havana Calle Ocho: The first Friday night of the month there's a free street festival with live music, food, and art. Stop by Ball & Chain (bar + salsa music) any night.
  • Everglades National Park: 45 min from downtown, Florida's unique wetland ecosystem. Airboats are the most exciting way to explore. Packages from $30/person.

Where to stay in Miami

South Beach (for the classic tourist)

Ocean Drive and Collins Avenue have hotels from $150 up to $800/night. The most popular in the mid-range: The Catalina Hotel, Pestana South Beach, Shore Club. Book 3+ months ahead during high season (Dec-Apr). Many South Beach hotels charge a resort fee ($30-50/night extra) that doesn't show up in the initial price.

Brickell (for the executive traveler)

The Kimpton Epic Hotel with views of Biscayne Bay is the business traveler's favorite. Four Seasons and Mandarin Oriental are the most luxurious options. Prices from $200/night. Less hectic than South Beach and with the Metrorail to get around without a car.

Doral (the most affordable and practical)

Hotels in Doral cost 40-50% less than in South Beach. Marriott, Hilton Garden Inn, Hyatt Place, and Hampton have modern properties at reasonable prices ($90-160/night). Ideal if you're mainly going to explore Miami and the Everglades, not to live on the beach. 10 minutes from MIA airport.

When to go to Miami from Texas?

  • December–April (high season): The weather is perfect — dry, temperatures of 68-82°F, ideal for the beach. It's the most expensive and crowded time. Book your hotel 2-3 months ahead.
  • May–June: Warm weather, still little rain, fewer tourists, and prices 20-30% lower. The best value for going from Texas.
  • July–September: Extreme heat and hurricane season. Very low prices but unpredictable weather — heavy afternoon rain. Good for those who can handle the heat and want to save.
  • October–November: Temperatures drop to more pleasant levels, tourism decreases, and prices are moderate. An excellent time to go if you can.

Combine Miami with Disney: the perfect Florida combo

Miami and Orlando are just 3.5 hours apart by highway (I-95 North). Many Texans do the perfect combo: fly to Miami, spend 2-3 days exploring the city, rent a car, and drive to Orlando for Disney World or the other parks. Then fly back to Texas from Orlando. The one-way car rental between Miami and Orlando costs $30-60 extra. This combination lets you see two of Florida's best cities in a single trip.

Essential tips for Miami

  • You can get around without a car: Uber and Lyft work great in Miami. The Metromover (free) connects Downtown, Brickell, and Edgewater. For South Beach ↔ Downtown, take the SoBe Free Trolley (free).
  • Latin food is the best and cheapest option: Calle Ocho in Little Havana, the walk-up windows in Doral, and the food trucks in Wynwood give you better meals than South Beach restaurants at half the price.
  • Miami comes alive at night: South Beach restaurants don't fill up until 9pm, and clubs open at midnight. If you want to get into a popular club (Liv, E11even), book a table ahead or arrive before 11pm.
  • Miami's sun is different: Even if you're coming from Texas (which is also hot), the combination of humidity + water reflection intensifies the sun. SPF50+ sunscreen, reapplied every 2 hours at the beach.
  • Tipping: The standard in Miami is 20-22% at restaurants. Many already add it automatically to the bill — check before leaving an additional tip.
  • Parking in South Beach: Costs $3-5/hour at public beaches. City parking lots ($1-2/hour) are 2-3 blocks away. Don't leave visible items in the car.

Frequently asked questions about Miami from Texas

How much does a couples trip to Miami from Texas cost?

A 4-night trip for two people (flight + hotel in a moderate area) costs between $1,800 and $2,800. If you prioritize the beach and South Beach, the budget goes up. If you stay in Doral or Brickell, you can be comfortable for $1,500.

Do I need to rent a car in Miami?

Not essential if you stay in South Beach or Brickell. Uber and the free trolley cover the most common trips. If you want to visit the Everglades, Coral Gables, the Keys, or combine with Disney Orlando, renting is worth it. Cars from Miami cost $40-80/day.

Is Miami safe for tourists?

The tourist areas of South Beach, Wynwood, Brickell, and Coconut Grove are very safe. As in any big city, avoid areas like Overtown or Liberty City at night. Don't leave valuables visible in the car, and use the hotel safe for documents and cash.

Can I visit Miami and the Keys on the same trip?

Yes — Key West is 3.5 hours away via US-1, the most impressive over-the-ocean highway in the U.S. You can do the day trip in a rental car or take the Greyhound bus ($30 one way). With 5-6 days in Florida, Miami + Key West is a perfect combination.

Do I need a visa to go to Miami from Texas?

No. Miami is in Florida, within the United States. If your immigration status in the U.S. is already in order — valid visa, TPS, parole, permanent residency, or citizenship — you don't need any additional paperwork to fly to Miami. You just need valid ID for the airport (state ID or passport).

Is Miami a good option for Venezuelan and Latin American travelers?

Yes — it's probably the most familiar city for Latin Americans in the entire U.S. Doral, known as "Doralzuela," has the largest concentration of Venezuelans outside Venezuela (arepas, cachapas, tequeños, Venezuelan brands), and Little Havana carries decades of Cuban history. You hear Spanish on the street, in taxis, and in restaurants. For many travelers from Texas, Miami feels like going home.
